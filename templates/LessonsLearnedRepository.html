Applicability

Lessons learned are any experiences (positive or negative) gained from executing a project that should be taken into consideration for future projects. The purpose is to collectively accumulate wisdom in order to identify actions or decisions that will reduce potential risk and/or reinforce positive results.

Lessons learned can be identified and documented at any point during the project's life cycle, but more formal sessions are typically held near the completion of the project.

Collections of lessons learned are held in a lessons learned repository for future reference. Simple, locally-accessible repositories can be easily set up in an Excel spreadsheet; while more enterprise-wide repositories can be established using collaborative tools such as SharePoint.
